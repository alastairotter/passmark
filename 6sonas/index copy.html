A new dawn, or more of the same? A comparison of Cyril Ramphosa’s speech with Jacob Zuma’s

Just two days into his presidency, Cyril Ramaphosa gave his first State of the Nation Address in Parliament on February 16, 2018. It was generally well received. In fact he even seems to have managed to ignite a renewed sense of hope in a nation that is fed up with its government. He dedicated a substantial chunk of his speech to persuading South Africans to put “the era of discord, disunity and disillusionment” behind them. “A new dawn is upon us,” he said.

What does that mean? Working on the assumption that the number of words the president devotes to a topic is an indication of the level of importance he places on that topic, Passmark looked at President Ramaphosa’s first speech and visualised the 12 topics that got the most airtime. We then looked at how those same 12 topics featured in the last five SONA speeches presented by the former president, Jacob Zuma. Education has been in the top 12 since February 2014, except for one year, 2016, which was, ironically, the first SONA after the #FeesMustFall protests started on campuses around the country.
